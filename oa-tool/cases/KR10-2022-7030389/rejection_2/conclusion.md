### 2. Our suggestion

The examiner alleged that claims 1-20 and 31-52 do not have an inventive step over D1 and D2. Specifically, the examiner rejected claim 1 for lacking an inventive step over D1 and D2.

In order to overcome this rejection reason, we suggest amending claim 1 as follows:

1.	A method for wireless communication by an apparatus of a wireless communication device, comprising:
obtaining a resource unit (RU) for transmitting a physical (PHY) layer convergence protocol (PLCP) protocol data unit (PPDU) over a wireless medium, wherein the RU comprises a portion of a tone plan for a frequency spectrum and includes a set of contiguous tones spanning a first bandwidth of the selected RU;
mapping the set of contiguous tones of the selected RU to a set of non-contiguous tones distributed across a second bandwidth of the frequency spectrum using a tone mapping distance applicable to an entirety of the second bandwidth of the frequency spectrum, the second bandwidth associated with the set of non-contiguous tones being greater than the first bandwidth associated with the set of contiguous tones, wherein the tone mapping distance indicates a distance or spacing between each pair of adjacent tones of each set of non-contiguous and non-punctured tones; and
transmitting the PPDU over the set of non-contiguous tones distributed across the second bandwidth of the frequency spectrum.

This amendment is supported by paragraphs [00XX]-[00YY] of the PCT application. A similar amendment can be made for the other independent claims. For more details, please refer to our Proposed Claims.

Proposed claim 1 recites the features of "the second bandwidth associated with the set of non-contiguous tones being greater than the first bandwidth associated with the set of contiguous tones," "tone mapping distance applicable to an entirety of the second bandwidth of the frequency spectrum," and "wherein the tone mapping distance indicates a distance or spacing between each pair of adjacent tones of each set of non-contiguous and non-punctured tones." We think that D1 and D2 fail to teach or suggest these features of proposed claim 1.

D1 relates to `PPDU` transmission using `distributed RUs` and `tone mapping distance (DTM)`. In this regard, D1 discloses that `RUs` used for allocation can be mapped to a set of `distributed non-contiguous tones` having a `tone mapping distance` in the `frequency spectrum` (D1 [0057]-[0062], [0105]-[0109], [0117], [0121]). D1 further states that "The DTM may be configured for mapping contiguous tones of each RU in the tone plan to a corresponding set of non-contiguous and non-punctured tones distributed across the frequency spectrum" (D1 [0123]). However, D1 does not explicitly teach or suggest that the `second bandwidth` associated with the `set of non-contiguous tones` is *greater than* the `first bandwidth` associated with the `set of contiguous tones`. Furthermore, D1 does not explicitly teach that the `tone mapping distance` is applicable to an *entirety* of the `second bandwidth` as a global parameter, nor does it define the `tone mapping distance` as indicating a `distance or spacing between each pair of adjacent tones` of each `set of non-contiguous and non-punctured tones`. Thus, D1 fails to teach or suggest the features of "the second bandwidth associated with the set of non-contiguous tones being greater than the first bandwidth associated with the set of contiguous tones," "tone mapping distance applicable to an entirety of the second bandwidth of the frequency spectrum," and "wherein the tone mapping distance indicates a distance or spacing between each pair of adjacent tones of each set of non-contiguous and non-punctured tones" as recited in proposed claim 1.

D2 relates to `distributed RUs` and `tone mapping distance (DTM)`. In this regard, D2 discloses that `RUs` can be mapped to a set of `distributed non-contiguous tones` using a `tone mapping distance` (D2 [0086], [0120]-[0136]). D2 also states that "The DTM may be configured for mapping contiguous tones of each RU in the tone plan to a corresponding set of non-contiguous and non-punctured tones distributed across the frequency spectrum" (D2 [0128]). While D2 mentions that `pilot tones` may be "evenly spread over the channel bandwidth" (D2 [0142]), it does not explicitly teach that the `second bandwidth` associated with the `set of non-contiguous tones` is *greater than* the `first bandwidth` associated with the `set of contiguous tones`. Moreover, D2 suggests that "A minimum mapping distance... may depend on the logic RU size for the channel bandwidth" (D2 [0143]), which implies that the `DTM` may vary depending on the `RU` size, contradicting the concept of a single `DTM` applicable to an *entirety* of the `second bandwidth`. D2 also lacks the specific definition of `DTM` as indicating a `distance or spacing between each pair of adjacent tones`. Thus, D2 fails to teach or suggest the features of "the second bandwidth associated with the set of non-contiguous tones being greater than the first bandwidth associated with the set of contiguous tones," "tone mapping distance applicable to an entirety of the second bandwidth of the frequency spectrum," and "wherein the tone mapping distance indicates a distance or spacing between each pair of adjacent tones of each set of non-contiguous and non-punctured tones" as recited in proposed claim 1.

A similar argument can be made for the other independent claims. Therefore, if you approve of our suggestion, we will argue these points in an Argument, based on amended claims.
